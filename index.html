Matthew I. Miller

I am an MD student in the laboratory of Dr. Vijaya Kolachalama at Boston University/Boston Medical Center, where I predominantly study deep learning methods in neuroradiology. My main research interests are in computer vision and image processing, though I'm also fascinated by Bayesian statistics, causal inference, graph theory, and geometry. Before all else, though, I am a (developing!) physician hoping to pursue a career in interventional radiology. Translating fundamental advances in imaging science to clinical impacts is what I strive for, and math/computing empower me to try!

This website is a place for me to mess around with front-end development, and also keep records of my experiments/personal learning in data science. Over time, I hope it will host various tutorials and original simulations about nontraditional statistics, image processing, and computational tools in medicine.
